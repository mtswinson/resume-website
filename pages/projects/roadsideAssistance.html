Roadside Assistance

(Oct 2023 - Jan 2024)

Context

Developed a rule-based eligibility tool that was integrated into General Motors’ emergency support ecosystem. The tool assessed customer entitlement to roadside services based on coverage tier, location, and policy constraints. It worked with third-party roadside response services and General Motors’ Mobile Guardian app, enabling customers to initiate and manage roadside events directly from their devices.

Problem/Goal

General Motors required a real-time tool to determine customer eligibility for roadside assistance. Advisors and roadside technicians needed immediate access to accurate coverage data to dispatch appropriate services. The system had to accommodate complex coverage logic, regional rules, and policy exceptions while integrating with mobile platforms and external service providers.

Process

Designed and implemented backend logic to evaluate customer coverage against service tiers and packages. Built rule-based flows that accounted for geographic constraints, policy limitations, and service boundaries. Ensured seamless integration with internal and third-party systems.

Impact/Differentiator

Enhanced service accuracy and reduced friction during roadside events. Customers received faster, more precise support; advisors and roadside technicians gained clearer visibility into service entitlements. Mobile Guardian integration empowered users to initiate and handle service independently. The scalable rule engine laid the foundation for future automation and service expansion.

Reflection/Key Takeaway

This project marked a pivotal moment in my full-stack development career. I took ownership of backend logic, delivered under pressure, and expanded my proficiency with Azure and service architecture. It strengthened my backend development skills and ability to deliver reliable, production-ready solutions on time.
